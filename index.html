About

Systems Researcher, Game Developer, Cellist

I am an aspiring computer scientist passionate about distributed systems research, mathematics, and game development. I'm currently an undergraduate student in my senior year at the University of Michigan, where I'm studying computer science, cello performance, and mathematics. I'm also a member of the Ordered Systems Lab led by Professor Ryan Huang, where I'm developing an LLM agent able to diagnose bugs in large distributed systems. The project's objective is to increase reliability and accelerate development of existing systems by decreasing the time and effort developers spend chasing down bugs.

Music is a huge part of my life as well; I have been playing cello since I was 9 years old, and I'm currently studying with Richard Aaron at the University of Michigan School of Music, Theatre, and Dance. Recordings of some of my favorite performances can be found below in the Performances section.
